Configure once, inherit everywhere

One field. Every surface. Zero rework.

Field types in CernoFlow aren't scalars with a display hint — they're full contracts. Each one defines its form control, its grid renderer, its storage shape, its PDF/A representation, its audit trail, its GDPR classification, and its mobile capture mode. Once.

Add a field to an entity and the platform propagates it to every surface that derives from the model — forms, audit views, sealed records, mobile capture, partner integrations. The Studio Co-designer can add it for you, and the change itself is recorded as a governed event.

Lifecycle & transitions

Lifecycles you can defend to a regulator.

Every entity has a lifecycle. Every transition is a contract — preconditions, role-bound actors, dual control where you need it, gateways that block until conditions are met, events fired to downstream systems on success. Configure transitions in the model, not in code.

Whether it's an approval, an onboarding, a case escalation, or a status change on a piece of equipment — the same governance rigour applies, and the platform records who decided what, when, and why. Compensating transactions on rollback. No silent deletes.

Auditability

Every action recorded. Nothing destroyed.

Every action a user or AI Agent takes generates an immutable event in the audit store — with dual attribution when an Agent participated, cryptographic sealing at terminal stages, and field-level GDPR classification at extraction.

Rollback never deletes. It records a compensating transaction that undoes the original action while preserving the original record. The checksum chain holds. When a regulator asks "who decided this and why," the platform answers without you having to assemble it from logs.

AI Agents

AI participates. The platform records.

AI Agents are participants in your processes — not a bolt-on assistant pane. They design entities with the implementer, assist case workers, guide external users, monitor SLAs, and recommend decisions. Every action they take generates an ai_action_capture row in the event store.

Attended

Acts inside your session.

The Studio Co-designer building entities with the implementer. The Case Agent helping a worker compare prior cases. You see and approve every step.

Delegated

Acts on your authorisation.

"Triage this batch by policy." "Draft the response and queue it for review." You authorise the scope, the Agent acts within it, the platform records every action.

Autonomous

Acts on schedule or trigger.

An SLA monitor that escalates when a case stalls. A reconciliation agent that fires nightly. Opt-in per Actor and per transition — both flags must be set before an Agent acts unattended.

Every Agent action is captured as a governed event. Same rigour, same audit trail, same compensating-transaction safety as a human action.
